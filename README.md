# Seminar Paper – AI-Based Cybersecurity Approaches for SMEs

This repository contains the electronic appendix for my seminar paper on **AI-based cybersecurity approaches for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)**.

The seminar paper was written as part of the seminar **Wirtschaftsinformatik 1** at the **University of Stuttgart**, **Institute of Business Administration**, **Department VII: Information Systems 1**.

## Topic

**AI as a Challenge for Cybersecurity in SMEs**

The paper investigates how AI-based approaches change and extend traditional cybersecurity measures in small and medium-sized enterprises.

## Methodology

The paper is based on a **systematic literature review (SLR)** conducted in accordance with **vom Brocke et al. (2009)**. The literature review was additionally classified using the taxonomy of **Cooper (1988)**.

The literature search was carried out in the databases **Scopus**, **EBSCOhost**, **IEEE Xplore**, **ACM Digital Library**, and **AISeL**. The search focused on scientific publications at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and small and medium-sized enterprises.

The following Boolean search string was used across the selected databases and applied to title, abstract, and keywords:

```text
("artificial intelligence" OR "machine learning" OR "AI") AND ("cybersecurity" OR "information security") AND ("small and medium enterprise*" OR "SME*")
```

The study selection followed a structured screening process based on predefined inclusion and exclusion criteria. The final literature base was analyzed using a **concept-oriented approach according to Webster and Watson (2002)**. The coding data and concept matrix provided in this repository document the analytical process.

## Results

The analysis shows that AI-based approaches do not replace traditional cybersecurity measures, but extend them in four main dimensions:

- **Analytical extension:** AI supports data-driven detection of anomalies, phishing attempts, malware, suspicious URLs, files, and network behavior.
- **Operational extension:** AI supports alert triage, log analysis, prioritization, and incident response.
- **Organizational extension:** AI helps make complex security information more understandable and usable for SMEs with limited cybersecurity expertise.
- **Strategic extension:** AI can support governance, compliance, documentation, and cyber resilience processes.

Overall, the paper concludes that AI-based cybersecurity is most useful for SMEs when it is introduced gradually, focused on specific use cases, integrated into existing processes, and kept under human control.
